# 📅 Seasonal Bug Alerts by Region

> Stay informed! Bug conditions change seasonally. This file tracks when each region sees the most (and least) insect activity. Update this as the community reports new data.

---

## 🌎 Africa

| Region | High Bug Season | Low Bug Season | Key Alerts |
|--------|----------------|----------------|------------|
| Sub-Saharan Africa (Safari) | Nov–Mar (wet season) | Jun–Oct (dry season) | ⚠️ Tsetse flies, malaria mosquitoes — NOT recommended for severe bug phobia |
| East Africa (coastal) | Year-round | Jul–Sep (cooler) | Mosquitoes persistent near coast |
| Southern Africa (Karoo, Namib) | Nov–Feb (rare) | Year-round generally | Desert regions very low bugs |

**Community Alert:** 🚨 SAFARI destinations (Kruger, Serengeti, Masai Mara) are NOT bug-free — mosquitoes are abundant. Consider South African/coastal desert regions instead for similar wildlife with fewer bugs.

| Region | High Bug Season | Low Bug Season | Key Alerts |
|--------|----------------|----------------|------------|
| Japan — Cities | May–Sep (humid) | Mar–May, Oct–Nov | ✅ Urban areas are well-screened/AC |

| Indian subcontinent | Jul–Sep (monsoon) | Oct–Mar | ⚠️ High mosquito + disease risk |
| Middle East (Gulf/Arabian Peninsula) | May–Sep (extreme heat) | Oct–Apr | ✅ Desert = very few bugs |
| Central Asia (Mongolia) | Jun–Aug (riverbanks) | Sep–May | ✅ Gobi/steppe = very few bugs |

**Community Alert:** 🚨 SE ASIA has the highest insect/disease risk of any region. Bug-phobic travelers should stick to Japan cities, Middle East, or Mongolia/Gobi.
