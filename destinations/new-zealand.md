# 🇳🇿 New Zealand — Clean, Green, and Crawl-Free

**Bug Level:** 🟢 Very Low — no mosquitoes, no dangerous spiders (katipo is rare and avoidable), minimal flies in rural areas
**Contributor:** Community Research
**Last Updated:** 2025

---

## Why It's Bug-Free

- New Zealand has **no native poisonous snakes, no dangerous spiders**, and very few biting insects
- The famous **katipo spider** (the only dangerously venomous spider) is rare, endangered, and found only in a few coastal sand dune areas — easily avoided
- **Strict biosecurity laws** prevent invasive insect species from establishing — a global model for bug-free travel
- The vast majority of your trip will be completely insect-free
- Island isolation has kept New Zealand free of many pests that plague other countries

---

## Best Time to Visit

### ❄️ Winter (June – August)
- **Temperature:** 5–10°C in lowlands; cold in the mountains
- **Bug Activity:** At their absolute minimum — virtually zero
- **Perks:** Fewer tourists, dramatic landscapes, geothermal areas steaming in the cold
- **Considerations:** Some roads in mountain areas may close due to snow; shorter days
- **Perfect for:** South Island glaciers, Rotorua geothermal parks, fiordland cruises

### 🌸 Spring/Summer (September – March)
- **Temperature:** 15–25°C (can reach 30°C in some areas)
- **Bug Activity:** Still very low — occasional **midges near still water** in summer (crane flies, not biting)
- **Perks:** Long days, blooming landscapes, all activities accessible
- **Considerations:** 
  - Midges near still/lake water in warmest weeks — carry a small portable fan or light repellent just in case
  - Peak tourist season (December-February)
- **Perfect for:** Hiking, beach trips, wildlife encounters, wine tours

### 🍂 Autumn (April – May)
- **Temperature:** 10–18°C
- **Bug Activity:** Declining — very few insects
- **Perks:** Stunning fall foliage, fewer tourists, great hiking weather
- **Perfect for:** Hiking the Routeburn Track, wine regions, photography

## Personal Travel Tips

- **The only insect you might see** is a crane fly (daddy long-legs) near still water in summer — they're completely harmless
- **Avoid the katipo spider** by sticking to maintained trails and sandy beaches (they live in dune grasses near the high-tide line)
- **Book accommodations with sealed windows and air conditioning** — standard in NZ hotels and motels
- **Carry a small portable USB fan** — if you're near still water in summer, it's enough to keep any midges away
- **Sea breezes are your friend** — coastal areas have even fewer insects
- **New Zealand's biosecurity is world-class** — respect the rules that keep it bug-free
- **Don't bring plant material or outdoor gear with soil** — it's illegal and could introduce invasive species

## ⚠️ Important Notes

- **Strict biosecurity**: You must declare all food, plant material, and outdoor gear at the border
- **Katipo spider**: Real but rare — lives in dune grasses near high tide; stay on tracks and you'll never see one
- **Midges in summer**: Near still water (lakes, ponds) in January-February — not biting, just annoying; a small fan solves this
- **No mosquitoes**: New Zealand has zero native mosquito species that bite humans
- **Modern accommodation**: Hotels, motels, and holiday parks all have sealed windows and AC
- **Island nation advantage**: New Zealand's isolation is its greatest bug-defence — respect the biosecurity that keeps it that way
