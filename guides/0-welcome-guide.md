## 📜 Community Rules

1. **Be kind and supportive** — No judgment about bug phobias. We get it.
2. **Data-driven only** — No "I think there were no bugs" without evidence. Use EPA, CDC, or reputable local sources.
3. **Always include seasonal context** — "Bug-free" only applies to certain months!
4. **Flag updates** — Climate change is moving bug ranges. If something changes, report it!
5. **No false guarantees** — Even the safest destinations can have surprises. Always recommend DEET as insurance.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Are there *any* places with truly zero bugs?
**A:** Yes! The only landmass with a **guaranteed** zero mosquito population is **Antarctica**. The Faroe Islands, Hokkaido (winter), and the Atacama Desert are considered 0/10 in their best seasons. Iceland was previously 100% mosquito-free but a species was discovered in Oct 2025 — verify before booking!

### Q: Do I still need DEET if I go to a "zero-bug" destination?
**A:** Pack it anyway! DEET is cheap insurance. Even Antarctica has springtails (non-biting). Climate change can push mosquito ranges into previously safe zones. Better safe than swatted!

### Q: Can I travel to "almost zero-bug" destinations if I have severe entomophobia?
**A:** It depends! A score of 1–2/10 means very rare encounters — you'd likely go a whole trip without seeing anything. But if even the *thought* of encountering a bug is distressing, stick to 0/10 destinations.

### Q: What about malaria zones?
**A:** Malaria = mosquitoes = danger for entomophobes. Avoid ALL malaria zones unless absolutely necessary. Check the [CDC Malaria Map](https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/maps/malaria) before booking.

### Q: Can I contribute even if I haven't traveled yet?
**A:** Absolutely! You can:
- Report bug sightings from your own area
- Help verify seasonal data with local sources
- Request new destinations for our research list
- Share tips from airports, hotels, etc.
