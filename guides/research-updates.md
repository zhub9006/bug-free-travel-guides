## ⚠️ Critical Corrections

### ⚠️ Seychelles is NOT Mosquito-Free
- **Reality:** Seychelles HAS mosquitoes year-round, especially Dec–Feb (rainy season)
- **Disease risk:** Malaria-free but dengue present; high mosquito density in wet season
- **Best safe window:** May–October with DEET as insurance
- **Correction date:** January 2026
- **Status:** ✅ Fixed in all references

### ⚠️ Iceland is No Longer 100% Mosquito-Free
- **Reality:** *Culiseta annulata* confirmed October 2025 in Kjós valley N of Reykjavík (2 females + 1 male)
- **Breeding status:** Unconfirmed at time of discovery; monitor Arctic Portal & Icelandic Nat. History before booking
- **Correction date:** February 2026
- **Impact:** Antarctica is now the ONLY guaranteed mosquito-free landmass
- **Status:** ✅ Fixed in all guides

### ⚠️ Patagonia: Winter ONLY
- **Reality:** November–March in Southern Hemisphere = aggressive sandflies; absolutely NOT for entomophobic travelers
- **Safe window:** ONLY June–August (their winter)
- **Correction date:** January 2026
- **Status:** ✅ Fixed in all guides

### ⚠️ Sahara Desert Added (July 2026)
- **Reality:** The Sahara is effectively zero-bug due to hyper-aridity (<1mm annual rainfall) and extreme heat
- **Bug score:** 0/10 — confirmed across multiple desert ecology sources
- **Best season:** November–February (mild temperatures 20-30°C)
- **Avoid:** June–September (extreme heat 45°C+, dangerous for travel)
- **Note:** Oases may have slightly more mosquitoes near water; avoid lingering at dusk
- **Correction date:** July 2026
- **Status:** ✅ Added to [Warm Bug-Free Destinations](9-warm-bug-free-destinations.md)

## 🔄 Research Methodology

All destination destinations in this repo follow a standardized verification protocol:

1. **Source Collection** — Gather information from at least 2 independent sources (government reports, scientific databases, travel forums, expert websites)
2. **Cross-Validation** — Verify bug-free claims against contradictory evidence
3. **Seasonal Analysis** — Determine best and worst visiting months based on climate data and mosquito activity patterns
4. **Expert Review** — Submit findings for community review before publishing
5. **Ongoing Monitoring** — Track climate anomalies, mosquito migrations, and new research that could affect destination profiles
