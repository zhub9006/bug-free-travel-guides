## 🚨 Seychelles Correction — NOT Truly Bug-Free

**UPDATE:** Several community sources and updated research indicate Seychelles **does have mosquitoes**, contrary to what some "bug-free" lists claim.

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Malaria** | ✅ Malaria-free (no Anopheles mosquitoes) |
| **Mosquitoes present?** | ⚠️ **Yes** — they exist, especially in rainy season (DEC–FEB) |
| **Dengue risk** | ⚠️ Occasional, more likely in rural areas |
| **Chikungunya** | ⚠️ Very rare but possible |
| **Best bug-free window** | May – October (dry season) — mosquitoes significantly reduced |
| **Actual experience** | Travelers in dry season report zero mosquito encounters; rainy season = peak activity |

> **Correction applied:** If traveling for severe entomophobia, Seychelles should be approached with DEET as insurance. Choose **May–October** and stay in air-conditioned/accommodation. Do NOT rely on it as mosquito-free.

**Source:** NatsTravel (2025); Island Hopper Guides (2025); Rustourism News (2025)

## 🚨 Iceland — Mosquito Discovery (Oct 2025)

**UPDATE:** Iceland is **no longer 100% mosquito-free**.

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Species** | *Culiseta annulata* (a cold-adapted mosquito) |
| **Location** | Kjós valley, N of Reykjavík |
| **Date** | October 2025 — confirmed by Icelandic Institute of Natural History |
| **Specimens found** | 2 female + 1 male |
| **Breeding population?** | Unconfirmed — threshold breached but established colony unverified |
| **Risk level** | Low but non-zero |
| **State before** | 100% mosquito-free (biological anomaly due to 3 annual freeze-thaw cycles) |
| **Climate change context** | Warmer winters may allow overwintering survival; currently monitored |

> **Recommendation:** Pack DEET when visiting Iceland. Monitor Icelandic Institute of Natural Health reports before traveling. Summer travel (JUN–AUG) remains safest; avoid Lake Mývatn area in midge season despite the name.

**Source:** Arctic Portal (Nov 2025); Travel & Leisure (Nov 2025); Earth.com (Nov 2025); Ourhotels.is (Feb 2026)

## ✅ New Caledonia — Enhanced Ecology (Issue: Underrepresented in README)

New Caledonia deserves far more than a single-line mention. Key ecological factors making it exceptionally bug-resistant:

| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| **Geographic Isolation** | ~1,200 km east of Australia; extreme island biogeographic distance |
| **Volcanic Soil Barriers** | High sulfur and mineral concentration leaches into water → inhospitable to larvae |
| **Endemic Plant Compounds** | Native flora evolved natural insect-repellent oils and saps to protect plants → incidentally suppresses mosquito populations |
| **Trade Winds** | Consistent oceanic breezes prevent stagnant water pooling |
| **Zero malaria/zoonotic disease** | Confirmed on main islands |
| **Dengue** | Very rare and localized |

> **See also:** `guides/3-new-caledonia-research.md` for full deep dive.
