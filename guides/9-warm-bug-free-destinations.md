## 1. 🏜️ Phoenix & Scottsdale, Arizona

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Bug score** | 1/10 (virtually zero year-round) |
| **Best season** | **October – April** — 70–85°F days, cool nights |
| **Avoid** | July–August (extreme heat 110°F+, brief monsoon = still very low bugs but uncomfortable) |
| **Why bug-free** | <100mm annual rainfall; hot-dry conditions suppress ALL insect breeding; massive urban irrigation is well-managed |
| **Mosquitoes** | Practically zero — even monsoon season barely registers |
| **Cockroaches** | Low (surprisingly dry climate naturally suppresses) |
| **Spiders/Ticks** | Very low — tarantulas occasional but reclusive |
| **Budget** | $$ (wide range from budget Airbnb to luxury resorts) |
| **Flight access** | Direct flights from all major US hubs via PHX |

**Top experiences:** Desert Botanical Garden, Camelback Mountain hiking, Sedona day-trip (45 min), Old Town Scottsdale, Tabac natural area.

**Travel tip:** Even during monsoon season (July–Aug), bug activity barely registers — brief rain is absorbed quickly by hardpan clay.

## 2. 🎰 Las Vegas, Nevada

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Bug score** | 1/10 (essentially zero in urban core) |
| **Best season** | **October – April** — pleasant 60–80°F days |
| **Avoid** | May–September (45°C+ heat but still very low bugs in Strip corridor) |
| **Why bug-free** | Same desert mechanics as Phoenix; Mojave Desert air; Las Vegas Valley is dry and well-drained |
| **Cockroaches** | Very low in tourist corridors; standard for any city |
| **Budget** | $$–$$$ (can be very affordable off-Strip or expensive on Strip) |
| **Flight access** | Direct flights from all major US hubs via LAS |

**Top experiences:** The Strip (walkable, air-conditioned), Red Rock Canyon (beautiful drive, fewer hikers = fewer bugs), Hoover Dam, Valley of Fire State Park.

**Travel tip:** Stay on the Strip or in major resort corridors — these areas are meticulously maintained with no standing water or insect breeding sites.

## 3. 🏝️ Canary Islands, Spain

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Bug score** | 2/10 (very low — arid volcanic terrain) |
| **Best season** | **November – April** — warm sun (~20–25°C), virtually zero bugs |
| **Avoid** | August (peak tourist season, slightly more midges in irrigated areas) |
| **Why bug-free** | Arid volcanic terrain at 2,000–6,400 ft elevation; constant trade winds prevent humidity buildup; NE Atlantic location prevents tropical conditions |
| **Mosquitoes** | Very low — lacks humid tropical conditions needed for breeding |
| **Spiders** | Very low — occasional harmless harvestman indoors only |
| **Cockroaches** | Low — drier climate suppresses tropical pest species |
| **Budget** | $$ — significantly cheaper than mainland Europe in winter |
| **Flight access** | Direct flights from US, UK, and mainland Europe (Tenerife, Gran Canaria, Lanzarote all have international airports) |

**Top experiences:** Teide National Park (highest peak in Spain, above cloud line = virtually bug-free), Maspalomas Sand Dunes (desert-like, near-zero bugs), Garajonay National Park (Laurel forest — stick to trails), swimming with dolphins.

**Best islands for bug-phobes:** Lanzarote (driest), Fuerteventura (arid), Tenerife (more variation but still low in tourist areas).

**Travel tip:** Avoid the lush laurel forests (Garajonay interior) if you're severe — stick to the coastal and highland resort areas.

## 4. ✨ Azores, Portugal — Misty Zero-Bug Atlantic Islands

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Bug score** | 0.5/10 (entire archipelago confirmed mosquito-free) |
| **Best season** | **Year-round** (minimal bugs even in summer) |
| **Weather** | Mild subtropical oceanic — 14–25°C; lush but breezy |
| **Why bug-free** | Strong Atlantic breezes; volcanic steep terrain limits standing water; isolation prevents colonization; no documented mosquito populations |
| **Caution** | Small midge presence near ponds in July — coastal wind eliminates quickly |
| **Budget** | $$ (significantly cheaper than mainland Portugal) |
| **Flight access** | Direct from US Northeast; 3.5 hr from Lisbon (LIS) |

**Top experiences:** Sete Cidades twin crater lakes, Furnas Valley hot springs, Gorreana tea plantation (bug-free strolls), whale watching off Pico, São Miguel hot springs.

**Best island:** São Miguel (most developed, easiest flights) — try Sete Cidades and Furnas.

**Travel tip:** Midges near ponds in July are the only real concern — just stay on paths near wind-exposed viewpoints and you'll be fine.

## 5. 🌺 Kona Coast, Hawaii — Breezy Leeward Side

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Bug score** | 2/10 (Kona coast specifically noted as driest/breeziest in Hawaii) |
| **Best season** | **April – October** — dry Trade Wind season |
| **Avoid** | Hilo side (wet side) — clearly more mosquitoes and bugs |
| **Why bug-free** | Acidic volcanic land; little standing water; strong Kona (Pacific) trade winds; southern leeward position |
| **Mosquitoes** | Concentrated on wet Hilo/Puna sides — **avoid these areas** |
| **Sandflies** | Low-moderate on lava fields at dawn/dusk — pack DEET for coastline sunrise walks |
| **Budget** | $$–$$$ (more affordable than other Pacific islands) |
| **Flight access** | Direct flights from US West Coast via KOA airport |

**Top experiences:** Kona coffee farms (bug-free!), South Point coastline, Puʻuhonua o Hōnaunau National Park, snorkel Kealakekua Bay (breezy = safe), Captain Cook Monument.

**Travel tip:** ONLY stay on the Kona (west) coast or Kohala district. If you end up on the Hilo/Puna side, expect mosquito swarms — they love the wet rainforest.

## 6. 🏔️ Puebla, Mexico — High-Altitude Bug-Free City

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Bug score** | 3/10 (protected by altitude) |
| **Best season** | **November – April** (dry season, 12–22°C, perfect temps) |
| **Why bug-free** | 2,100 m / 7,000 ft elevation — cool mountain plateau climate directly prevents mosquito establishment |
| **Caution** | Stray cockroaches in older buildings (standard for any Mexican city — not a landscape concern) |
| **Budget** | $ (very affordable!) |
| **Flight access** | Direct from Mexico City (1 hr drive) — no international flights direct |

**Top experiences:** Baroque cathedral (UNESCO), Talavera pottery workshops, Cholula pyramid (world's largest), Iztaccíhuatl volcano hiking, mole poblano cuisine.

**Travel tip:** Puebla is an excellent alternative to beach Destinations in Mexico — cooler, safer from bugs, and far cheaper than Cancún or Riviera Maya.

**⚠️ Avoid:** Oaxaca state (much lower elevation, much more bugs).
