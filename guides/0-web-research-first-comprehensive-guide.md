### 🏝️ 10. CAYMAN ISLANDS — Limestone Fortress at Sea Level

**Bug Score: 1/10** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Why bugs aren't a problem** | The Cayman Islands are flat, low-lying **limestone atolls** (no rivers, no freshwater natural habitats anywhere). The porous limestone substrate drains instantly — there is simply nowhere for water to stagnate. These geological conditions are perfect for **natural mosquito exclusion**. Plus, aggressive mosquito-control programs: regular larviciding of drains, community-wide spraying cycles, and strict hotel/resort sanitation standards. The Cayman Islands are **not tropical rainforest** (unlike Jamaica, Cuba, or Dominican Republic) — they are essentially a raised reef platform with xeriscaped vegetation. |
| **Best months to visit** | **December – April** (perfect: 24-28°C, dry, minimal humidity). Also excellent year-round due to instant limestone drainage. |
| **Worst months** | **September – October** (hurricane risk + more rain; but still minimal mosquitoes since limestone absorbs water instantly). |
| **Peak bug season** | ❌ None — bug-proof geology |
| **Budget Tier** | 💰💰💰 (mid-range: seaside apartments, local restaurants) |
| **Best for** | Seven Mile Beach, world-class scuba diving, tax-free shopping, Stingray City, Bat Cave, just being gloriously bite-free |
| **Top spots** | **Grand Cayman:** Seven Mile Beach, George Town, Hell, Rum Point; **Cayman Brac:** limestone cliffs, caves; **Little Cayman:** pristine diving |

The Kona coffee belt (elevation 500-1,200m) is naturally ventilated. **Key cities like Kailua-Kona, Waikoloa, Waimea, and Captain Cook** have minimal mosquito activity year-round compared to Hilo, Honolulu, or Lihue on other islands. |
