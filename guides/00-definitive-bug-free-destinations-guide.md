> ## 📊 How to Read Bug Scores
>
> Every destination below is rated on a **0–10 bug scale**:
>
> | Score | Label | What It Means | DEET Needed? |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | **0/10** | 🛡️ Zero Bug Zone | No biting insects. Period. | No (pack as backup insurance) |
> | **0.5/10** | ⭐ Near-Zero | Practically zero — one in a million chance | Optional |
> | **1–2/10** | ✅ Very Low | Rare mosquitoes in specific spots/times | Recommended |
> | **3–4/10** | ⚪ Low | Manageable with DEET + long sleeves at dawn/dusk | Yes |
> | **5+ /10** | 🚫 Not Recommended | Active mosquito populations, disease risk | Mandatory but still risky |

> ## 🧪 Why These Places Are Bug-Free — The Science
>
> Understanding **why** a destination has few bugs helps you assess risk:
>
> | Mechanism | Destinations That Benefit | Explanation |
> |---|---|---|
> | **Extreme Cold** | Antarctica, Svalbard, Greenland, Iceland, Lofoten | Temps below mosquito flight threshold (~10°C/50°F). No overwintering habitat. |
> | **Extreme Aridity** | Atacama, Dubai, Jordan, Wadi Rum, Phoenix | Standing water = mosquito breeding. No water = no mosquitoes. Period. |
> | **Island Isolation** | Montserrat, Curaçao, Faroe Islands, Socotra | Ocean barriers prevent colonization. Volcanic soil drains fast — no larvae habitat. |
> | **Oceanic Climate** | Canary Islands, Azores, Madeira, Faroe Islands | Consistent winds + cool temps = insects can't establish populations. |
> | **Volcanic Soil Drainage** | Iceland, Azores, Montserrat, New Caledonia | Water passes through volcanic rock instantly. No stagnant pools for larvae. |
> | **Urban Pest Control** | Dubai, Singapore (select areas), Tokyo, Phoenix/Las Vegas | Government-funded larviciding, sanitation, and building standards minimize urban bugs. |

> ## 💡 Essential Tips for Bug-Phobic Travelers
>
> ### Before You Book
> 1. **Check the season** — Even bug-free destinations can have seasonal spikes. Always verify the month-by-month bug forecast.
> 2. **Read the Research Updates** — Check [Research Updates & Corrections](guides/research-updates.md) for the latest before booking.
> 3. **Pack DEET as insurance** — Even our safest destinations have a <0.5% chance of an unexpected insect. Pack 30%+ DEET "just in case."
> 4. **Book accommodations with AC** — Air conditioning keeps rooms bug-free and creates a physical barrier.
> 5. **Request upper floors** — Mosquitoes stay low. Always request a 3rd floor or higher room.
>
> ### While Traveling
> 1. **Seal your room** — Close windows/doors, check screens, use door draft stoppers.
> 2. **Avoid dawn/dusk** — Even low-bug areas have peak insect activity at these times. Plan indoor activities then.
> 3. **Stick to paved/urban areas** — Bugs concentrate in wilderness, near water, and in tall grass.
> 4. **Check bedding** — Even in bug-free destinations, make a quick visual check of hotel sheets (bedbugs are global).
> 5. **Use mosquito netting** — Lightweight, packable, and provides psychological comfort even when unnecessary.
