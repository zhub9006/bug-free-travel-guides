# ❄️ Alaska — Bug-Free Arctic & Desert Escape

> Research-backed guide for bug-phobic travelers. Alaska is a paradox: famous for summer mosquitoes in the Interior, but **bug-free in many regions** if you pick the right spots and seasons.
>
> ⚠️ **AVOID Interior Alaska in July** — brutal mosquito season. This guide covers only the reliably bug-free zones.

---

| Region | Bug Score | Verdict | Best Season |

| **Seward Peninsula / Nome** | 1/10 | Very low | Jun–Aug |

## Top Bug-Free Spots

- **Utqiaġvik (Barrow)** — Polar bears, ice formations, Iñupiat culture, zero mosquitoes
- **Gates of the Arctic NP** — Remote wilderness, no roads, no bugs (above treeline)
- **Nome** — Gold rush history, Bering Sea coast, virtually no insects
- **Selawik NWR** — Remote tundra, birding, near-zero bugs
- **Kenai Fjords (Seward)** — Glaciers, whales, accessible from Anchorage, low bugs

## Pro Tips

1. **Skip Interior Alaska in July** — Visit Denali in May/June or Aug/Sep instead
2. **Elevation is your friend** — Above 2,500 ft in Denali, mosquitoes are virtually absent
3. **Arctic coast = bug-free haven** — Utqiaġvik and Anaktuvuk Pass are near zero year-round
4. **DEET insurance** — Carry a small bottle for rare dawn/dusk mosquitoes near streams
5. **No malaria, no dengue, no snakes** — Alaska is free of all vector-borne diseases

> **Bug-Free Tip:** Visit the Arctic coast in summer for polar bears under the midnight sun — not a single bug!
